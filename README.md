# Banking-Analysis

# 🏦 Banking Analytics Project

## 📌 Project Overview

This project presents an end-to-end analysis of banking customer and financial data using **Python, SQL, and Power BI**. The objective is to analyze customer demographics, deposits, loans, credit exposure, income groups, and loyalty classifications to generate meaningful business insights.

The project covers the complete analytics workflow:

- Data cleaning and exploratory data analysis using Python and Pandas
- Business problem solving using SQL
- Interactive dashboard development using Power BI
- Analysis of customer segments, deposits, loans, income, and financial risk

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies

- **Python** – Data cleaning and exploratory data analysis
- **Pandas** – Data manipulation and transformation
- **Matplotlib & Seaborn** – Exploratory data visualization
- **MySQL** – Business query analysis
- **Power BI** – Dashboard development and data visualization
- **DAX** – KPI and business measure creation

---

# 📊 Dashboard Overview

The Power BI dashboard contains three analytical pages:

1. Executive Summary
2. Customer Insights
3. Financial Performance

Each page focuses on a different business area while interactive filters allow users to analyze specific customer groups.

---

# 1️⃣ Executive Summary

## 📄 Page Overview

The Executive Summary provides a high-level view of the bank's overall business performance. It combines major financial KPIs with customer demographic information to give decision-makers a quick understanding of the bank's current position.

The page tracks total customers, deposits, credit exposure, credit card balances, average income, and average risk weighting. It also highlights the distribution of customers across gender, age, income, and nationality groups.

A detailed customer table allows users to identify high-value customers based on deposits and loans. Interactive filters make it possible to analyze specific loyalty, gender, nationality, and age segments.

---

## 📌 KPI Cards

### Total Customers

Displays the total number of customers in the bank's dataset. This KPI provides an immediate view of the overall customer base available for segmentation and financial analysis.

### Average Income

Shows the average estimated income of all customers. This helps the bank understand the overall earning capacity of its customer base and supports customer affordability analysis.

### Total Deposits

Represents the total amount of money deposited by customers. It is an important indicator of the bank's funding strength and customer savings behaviour.

### Total Credit Exposure

Measures the total credit provided to customers across different lending products. This KPI helps management understand the bank's overall lending exposure.

### Total Credit Card Balance

Shows the combined outstanding credit card balance across all customers. It helps assess customer dependence on revolving credit and overall unsecured credit exposure.

### Average Risk Weighting

Displays the average risk score across the customer portfolio. This helps management monitor the overall risk profile of the bank's customers.

# 🐍 Python Exploratory Data Analysis

Python and Pandas were used to explore and prepare the banking dataset before SQL analysis and dashboard development.

The EDA process included:

- Understanding dataset structure and data types
- Identifying missing and duplicate values
- Cleaning inconsistent data
- Creating useful customer categories
- Analyzing numerical distributions
- Comparing customer demographics
- Studying relationships between income, deposits, and loans
- Identifying outliers and unusual financial behaviour

The analysis helped validate the dataset and discover patterns before developing SQL business queries and Power BI visuals.

---

# 🗄️ SQL Business Analysis

SQL was used to solve business problems that require deeper analysis beyond standard dashboard visuals.

The analysis includes concepts such as:

- Aggregations and grouping
- Subqueries and Common Table Expressions (CTEs)
- Window functions
- Ranking functions
- Running totals
- Customer segmentation
- Percentage contribution analysis
- Conditional aggregation
- Top-N analysis

Example business problems include:

- Identifying customers in the top income segments with below-average deposits
- Measuring the percentage of total deposits controlled by top customers
- Ranking nationalities by average deposits within income categories
- Identifying customer segments contributing significantly to deposits but less to loans
- Finding high-value customers based on deposits and lending activity
- Comparing financial behaviour across age, income, nationality, and occupation groups

---

# 🔍 Key Business Insights

- Senior customers form the largest age segment and contribute significantly to overall banking activity.
- Mid-income customers represent the largest portion of the customer base.
- European customers form the largest nationality group in the dataset.
- Deposit and loan activity is heavily concentrated among senior and middle-aged customers.
- Credit card balances are significantly higher among senior customers.
- Income level does not always translate directly into higher savings behaviour.
- Customer loyalty classifications provide opportunities for targeted retention and premium service strategies.
